# Kasetsart University Game Development 2026 Course Project

Cooking Putter and The Regendary Gordon Busyman is a restaurant-management game created as part of the Game Development 2026 course.

In this game, players manage a small restaurant, cook meals, serve customers, clean dishes, and survive the arrival of the mysterious food critic known as Regendary Gordon Busyman.

## Features

- Restaurant management gameplay
- Cooking and food preparation system
- Customer AI and table management
- Dynamic food ordering system
- Dish cleaning and serving mechanics
- Day and night progression
- Special food critic event
- Story-based ending sequence
- Pixel-art inspired presentation
- Original animations and interactions
- Special Event

A mysterious evaluation from Regendary Gordon Busyman may alter reality itself.

Players who successfully satisfy the legendary critic may uncover an ending beyond human comprehension.

## Known Issues / Missing Features

Due to the limited development time and overlapping university deadlines, some planned features were not fully implemented.

Current limitations include:

- No in-game quit option/menu
- Shop upgrade system was planned but not implemented
- Some UI screens have limited controller support
  - Food selection menu
  - Ingredient purchasing menu
- Minor UI and interaction inconsistencies may occur
- Some UI elements are placeholder/simple in presentation
- - Limited tutorialization for gameplay systems

Despite these limitations, the core gameplay experience is fully playable from start to ending. (I hope XD)

## How To Play

### Day Phase — Town & Forest

- The player begins in the town scene
- The player can buy ingredient from shop
- The player can go into restaurant or Forest
- Gather ingredients by collecting resources from trees and other objects
- After collecting enough resources (around 12 trees), time will advance automatically
- The player will then be transported to the restaurant at night

### Night Phase — Restaurant

- Open the fridge to check available ingredients
- Select a food recipe to cook
- Press `H` to open the cookbook and view recipes/cooking steps
- The cookbook uses a scroll-view system, so players must scroll down to view additional recipes (It's digital book. Fr Fr)

### Cooking

Depending on the recipe, players may need to:
- Chop ingredients
- Grill food
- Fry food

Players should follow the recipe instructions shown in the cookbook.

### Serving Customers

- Deliver completed food to waiting customers
- Customers automatically pay after eating
- Dirty dishes must be collected manually
- Bring dirty dishes to the sink to wash them

### Advancing the Day

- Players may advance to the next day at any time by leaving the restaurant during nighttime
- The restaurant scene also contains a timer
- If the timer reaches zero, the game automatically advances to the next day

## Development Notes

The project was developed under a very limited schedule. Although the official development period lasted around 4 weeks, the team was simultaneously handling several other university projects during the same timeframe. (I HAVE 4 MORE PROJECTS AHHHHHH)

As a result:

Around half of the team worked on the project for approximately 2 weeks
The remaining members joined the development only a few days before the deadline (around 3–4 days)

Despite the time constraints, the team successfully completed:

- Core restaurant gameplay systems
- Customer AI and food ordering
- Cooking and serving mechanics
- Cleaning systems
- Day cycle progression
- Special event sequences
- Ending interactions and visual effects

The project reflects rapid iteration, teamwork under pressure, and a strong focus on delivering a complete playable experience within strict academic deadlines.


Developed for educational purposes as part of the Game Development 2026 curriculum at Kasetsart University.
